GJ Tech Management Project Course

Practical Exposure to Real-World Business Challenges

Course Introduction

The Management Project Course is designed to give students practical exposure to real-world business and management problems. It helps in applying classroom concepts such as planning, organizing, leadership, teamwork, and decision-making to practical projects. Through this course, students learn how to manage resources, analyze situations, solve problems, and deliver results effectively.

This course is a hands-on, project-based learning experience designed to bridge the gap between academic theory and professional practice. Students will work in teams on a real-world project, applying key management principles to achieve a successful outcome.

Course Objectives

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  • Apply Project Management Fundamentals: Understand and utilize the five project lifecycle phases: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Monitoring & Control, and Closure.
  • Develop Core Management Skills: Enhance abilities in planning, organizing, resource management, and budgeting.
  • Practice Leadership and Teamwork: Demonstrate effective leadership, communication, and conflict resolution within a project team.
  • Analyze and Solve Problems: Use critical thinking and analytical tools to identify project risks, analyze situations, and develop effective solutions.
  • Communicate Effectively: Prepare professional project proposals, reports, and presentations for various stakeholders.
  • Utilize Project Management Tools: Become proficient in using common project management software and techniques (e.g., Gantt Charts, WBS).

Module 1: Introduction to Project Management

Topics:

  • What is a project? The characteristics of projects vs. operations.
  • The strategic importance of projects in an organization.
  • The role and responsibilities of a project manager.
  • Introduction to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK) and other methodologies (Agile, Waterfall).

Activities:

  • Team formation and charter creation.
  • Brainstorming and selecting a project topic.

Module 2: Project Initiation and Scope Definition

Topics:

  • Conducting a feasibility study and stakeholder analysis.
  • Developing a project charter and a clear scope statement.
  • Defining project goals, objectives, and deliverables.
  • Creating a Work Breakdown Structure (WBS) to break down the project into manageable tasks.

Activities:

  • Drafting the Project Charter for the team project.
  • Presenting the project idea and scope to the class.

Module 3: Project Planning and Scheduling

Topics:

  • Developing a detailed project schedule (Gantt chart, Critical Path Method).
  • Estimating time, costs, and resources.
  • Creating a project budget and resource allocation plan.
  • Identifying and managing project dependencies.

Activities:

  • Creating a detailed project plan and schedule.
  • Using project management software (e.g., Microsoft Project, Asana, Trello) to build the schedule.

Module 4: Risk and Quality Management

Topics:

  • Identifying, analyzing, and mitigating project risks.
  • Developing a risk management plan.
  • Implementing quality assurance and quality control.
  • Understanding the importance of a quality management plan.

Activities:

  • Conducting a risk assessment for the team project.
  • Developing a risk register and mitigation strategies.

Module 5: Project Execution and Team Leadership

Topics:

  • Executing the project plan.
  • Leadership styles in a project environment.
  • Team building, motivation, and managing conflict.
  • Effective communication within the team and with stakeholders.

Activities:

  • Weekly team meetings and progress reports.
  • Case study analysis on leadership challenges.

Module 6: Monitoring, Control, and Change Management

Topics:

  • Tracking project progress against the plan.
  • Performance reporting and Earned Value Analysis (EVA).
  • Managing project changes and "scope creep."
  • Developing a change control process.

Activities:

  • Analyzing project performance data.
  • Submitting a formal Change Request for the team project (if applicable).

Module 7: Project Closure and Final Presentation

Topics:

  • The project closure process and final deliverables.
  • Conducting a "lessons learned" session.
  • Final project reporting and documentation.
  • Handover to the client or organization.

Activities:

  • Final project report submission.
  • Final project presentation to the class and a panel of "stakeholders."

Assessment

The course is heavily project-based, with assessments focusing on both the process and the final outcome.

Breakdown:

  • Project Deliverables (60%):
    • Project Charter & Scope Document
    • Project Plan & Budget
    • Risk & Quality Management Plan
    • Progress Reports
    • Final Project Report & Documentation
  • Team Performance (20%):
    • Peer evaluations based on contribution, communication, and teamwork.
  • Individual Assessments (20%):
    • Mid-term quiz on project management concepts.
    • Final presentation (individual grade).
    • Reflective journal on personal learning and challenges.
